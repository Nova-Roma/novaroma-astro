Advanced manufacturing and cyber-physical systems do not respect borders; neither should the pathways that prepare workers to operate and improve them. The coalition aligns employers, unions, polytechnics, and standards bodies around **stackable credentials** and shared competency language.

Cohorts move through blended modules—sensing and data, human–machine collaboration, maintenance ethics, and line-level innovation—then apply learning in partner facilities with mentorship. Communicative, team-based training is built in so tacit knowledge transfers alongside formal skills.

Members commit to multi-year cooperation: joint curriculum pilots, recognition of prior learning, and policy dialogues on sovereignty in supply chains and critical infrastructure. The aim is resilience that outlasts single funding cycles—**vertical integration of talent and trust**, not one-off workshops.
