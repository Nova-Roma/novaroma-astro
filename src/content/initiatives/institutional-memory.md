When people leave or systems change, institutions lose more than files—they lose the quiet reasoning behind decisions. This initiative builds rituals and lightweight archives so stories, constraints, and “why we stopped doing that” survive handoffs without burying newcomers in noise.

We pair oral history sprints with structured decision logs and role shadowing. Young colleagues contribute fresh synthesis; senior staff anchor authenticity. The goal is less documentation for its own sake, and more **shared context** that reduces rework and makes the mission legible.

Pilot partners include public agencies and research institutes testing intergenerational cohorts. Open calls emphasize projects where a little awe—seeing how past breakthroughs happened—helps new talent imagine what they can build next.
