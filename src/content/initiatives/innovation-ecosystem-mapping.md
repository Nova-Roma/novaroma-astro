You cannot strengthen what you cannot see. Regional teams combine interviews, open data, and participatory workshops to produce **maps**: who funds whom, where ideas stall, which bridges are missing between academia, industry, and community.

Deliverables range from printed atlases for policymakers to living dashboards maintained by local stewards. Mapping sprints surface blind spots—duplicate programmes, underused labs, corridors where trust is thin—so partners can coordinate instead of competing blindly.

The **board-game deck** translates the same vocabulary into tactile play: roles, resources, events, and “system shocks” that mirror real ecosystems. Facilitators use it in classrooms, incubators, and cross-sector retreats to teach innovation dynamics in an hour—and leave behind relationships that outlast the game.
